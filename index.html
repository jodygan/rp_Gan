Isopropanol's effect on Rhodococcus rhodochrous degrading Polyethylene Terephthalate

Sophomore Year Research Project



Rationale

The environment and human lives are at risk due to plastic pollution. Not only do animals consume the plastic, but the land they live in is being disturbed. Microplastics can also harm many systems in the human body. Current recycling processes for plastics are not ideal, as they use toxic chemicals that are not environment friendly. With rising plastic pollution levels, microbial degradation can offer a timely and sustainable solution.



Background

The Rhodococcus genus encompasses bacteria with degradation capabilites. Recents studies have discovered that the genus can degrade polyethylene teraphthalate (PET) film and BTEX compounds. PET plastic is widely used in single-use products such as water bottles and food packaging. Its inability to naturally degrade accumulates plastic waste. However, the plastic continues to be used due to its characteristics. They include: impact strength, durability, and temperature resistance.



Research

This study consisted of two components: the growth of R. rhodochrous and the effect of isopropanol on R. rhodochrous growth/degradation. The amount of isopropanol added to each culture served as the independent varaible. There were experimental groups that tested the optimal growth conditions for the bacteria. All experimental trials grew R. rhodochrous in Luria Broth (LB). Once the optimal conditions were found, cultures were grown meeting said conditions. Isopropanol and prepared plastic samples was then added to the new cultures.
